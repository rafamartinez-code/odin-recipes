Chocolate

Description

This recipe creates rich and creamy chocolate that melts in your mouth. Perfect for snacking, gifting, or using in your favorite desserts

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil (or cocoa butter for a richer taste)
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Prepare Your Workspace: Ensure your work area is clean and dry. Water can cause the chocolate to seize. Line a baking sheet or a chocolate mold with parchment paper.
  2. Melt the Coconut Oil (or Cocoa Butter): In a double boiler or a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water, melt the coconut oil (or cocoa butter). Stir occasionally until completely melted.
  3. Mix in Cocoa Powder: Sift the cocoa powder to remove any lumps. Gradually add the sifted cocoa powder to the melted oil, stirring continuously until smooth and well combined.
  4. Add Sweeteners and Flavorings: Add the powdered sugar, a little at a time, and stir until fully dissolved. You can adjust the amount of sugar based on your taste preference. Stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt. Mix well.
  5. Pour and Set: Pour the chocolate mixture into the prepared baking sheet or molds. Use a spatula to spread it evenly if necessary. If desired, sprinkle sea salt flakes or additional toppings on top of the chocolate.
  6. Chill the Chocolate:Place the chocolate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or until it is completely set and firm.
  7. Break and Serve: Once set, break the chocolate into pieces if you used a baking sheet, or pop them out of the molds. Store the chocolate in an airtight container in the refrigerator to prevent melting.
